A frame is basically the ordered list of basis vectors, that may be attached to a body, making it a body frame. When we refer to a body frame {b}, we mean a motionless frame that is instantaneously coincident with a frame that is fixed to a (possibly moving) body.

A vector can be thought of as the product of a row matrix containing basis vectors of a frame, and a column matrix containing coordinates.

Rotation matrices can be used to describe the orientation of a body frame w.r.t. another. The basis vectors of that frame, as expressed in observer frame’s coordinate system form the columns of the rotation matrix.

For now, v\bold{v}v﻿ and v⃗\vec{v}v﻿ are considered different, first one is an array of numbers, second is a physical vector. But soon we will be using these interchangeably.

Such matrices give us a way to find the representation of a vector in a frame, based on the representation in a different frame.
